Research Domain

Bridging the Accessibility Gap in ICT Education

Literature Survey

Our comprehensive literature survey analyzed over 100 research publications focusing on assistive technologies, multimodal interaction, and STEM education for the visually impaired. Key areas of investigation included:

  • Efficacy of haptic-auditory feedback in navigation.
  • Challenges in teaching spatial logic (Logic Gates/Tables) to blind learners.
  • Current limitations of traditional screen readers (NVDA/TalkBack) in ICT pedagogy.
  • Machine learning applications in identifying user navigational behavioral patterns.

Domain Insights

Primary Focus: G.C.E. O/L ICT Syllabus (Grade 10/11)

Target Users: Visually Impaired Students & ICT Teachers

Key Tech: Multimodal AI & Edge Computing

Survey Status: Completed

Research Gap Analysis

Gap 1: Diagram Spatiality

Traditional assistive tools lack the ability to interpret the spatial structure of ICT diagrams (Logic Gates and Truth Tables), making it difficult for VI students to conceptualize logical flows.

Gap 2: Real-time Assistance

Existing systems do not proactively identify when a student is struggling with gesture-based navigation, leading to high frustration and abandonment of the digital tool.

Gap 3: Offline Resilience

Current AI-driven accessibility solutions rely heavily on cloud connectivity, which is highly unreliable in rural Sri Lankan regions, creating a digital divide.

Research Problem

Problem Statement

Visually impaired students in Sri Lanka face significant barriers in mastering O/L ICT due to the visual-centric nature of the syllabus. The lack of low-latency, offline-capable assistive tools that can interpret complex diagrams and adapt to user struggle prevents independent and inclusive learning.

Core Challenges

  • High cognitive load in interpreting non-textual data.
  • Inefficient gesture interaction without adaptive guidance.
  • Unreliable internet connectivity in rural educational settings.

Research Objectives

Primary Objectives

  • Develop an edge-based RFC model for gesture struggle detection.
  • Implement a multimodal (Haptic/Audio) navigation framework.
  • Design an AI-driven diagram-to-voice interpretation module.
  • Create a predictive analytics dashboard for teachers.

Secondary Objectives

  • Ensure 100% offline functionality for core learning modules.
  • Validate system efficacy with the Employers' Federation of Ceylon (EFC).
  • Optimize low-latency performance using client-side JavaScript logic.

Research Methodology

Research Approach

Type: Mixed Methods (Qualitative & Quantitative)

Technique: Experimental & Case Study (EFC Setting)

Key Phases:

  1. User Requirement Gathering (VI Students/Teachers)
  2. Model Training (10,000+ Gesture Data Points)
  3. Agile Software Development (React Native)
  4. Pilot Testing & Struggle Analysis
  5. Refinement using Behavioral Metrics (XGBoost)
